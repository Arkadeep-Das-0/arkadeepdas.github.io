I'm a computer scientist based in Dhaka, Bangladesh, moving into cybersecurity research after a first chapter spent building production systems and publishing in applied machine learning. My interests centre on software and network security, cloud security, and the security of systems built on large language models.

The path here is deliberate. As an undergraduate researcher at BRAC University I designed and trained deep sequence models — publishing two peer-reviewed IEEE papers on genome-mutation forecasting and medical image analysis — which taught me how to carry a research problem end to end. At Samsung R&D Institute Bangladesh I shipped enterprise software and got hands-on with access control and endpoint restriction, and at Fiber@Home I worked inside a live IP transmission network, troubleshooting routing, switching, and traffic. ML rigour, production engineering, and real network experience — that's the combination I want to bring to security.

I'm currently a research assistant at Elite Research Lab, running literature review and early problem exploration to sharpen my direction, and applying to PhD programs for 2026.
